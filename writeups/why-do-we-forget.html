Why Do We Forget?

We have all, at least once, forgotten an answer in an exam. We all tend to forget things every now and then. Why do we forget certain things so easily? What is the answer to the unforgettable question: “Why do we forget?”

Memories are like little snapshots or mental records of our experiences from the past. When we perform certain activities, those memories are first stored in our short-term memory. Then, our brain makes a decision about which memories are important and which are not.

Important information is transferred to long-term memory through the hippocampus, the part of the brain that helps process memories. The rest is left behind in short-term memory and eventually forgotten. To remember something, we sometimes have to repeat it to ourselves a couple of times so that it stays with us.

Unimportant information is removed from our brain, while information that matters to us is transferred to long-term memory and remembered for longer. For example, try recalling a family function or your friend’s birthday party from last year. Now try recalling the outfit you wore a week ago. Many of us would find the second one much harder to remember.

To improve our memory and keep our brain healthy, it is important to stay physically active through exercise. It is also important to eat healthy food and give the brain a good workout by solving puzzles and exposing it to new challenges.
