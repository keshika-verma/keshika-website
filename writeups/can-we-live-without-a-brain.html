Can We Live Without a Brain?

Our brain is one of the most important parts of our body, controlling almost all body functions. How much of a brain do you think you could live without? A quarter? Half? No brain at all?

That does sound really weird, but in some extraordinary cases, it is possible.

Our brain is a deeply complex organ. It is divided into parts such as the cerebrum, which controls our emotions and learning ability, and the cerebellum, which controls muscle movement and balance. But the brain’s function is not limited only to the body. It also plays a major role in shaping our personality.

The best example of this is Phineas Gage. Phineas Gage, a railroad worker, once accidentally impaled his skull with a 43-inch-long rod while working. And yet, he survived. After this incident, however, his social behavior changed drastically. He was no longer the same Gage.

Another unusual case occurred in 2014, when a 24-year-old Chinese woman went to a hospital complaining about dizziness. With the help of a CT scan, doctors discovered that she had been born without a cerebellum. And yet, she had lived a good part of her life normally, without even knowing anything about it.

Talking about even more extreme conditions, in 2001, Trevor Waltrip was born without a forebrain, and his cranial cavity was filled mostly with cerebrospinal fluid. Even without major parts of the brain, he lived for 12 years. Although he did not have basic brain components, his brain stem was intact. This part of the brain helped him breathe, maintain a heartbeat, and perform some very basic functions.

So, in conclusion, living with part of the brain missing is possible because other parts of the brain can sometimes adapt to the functions of the missing parts over time. This ability is called neuroplasticity.

In fact, you may be surprised to know that even simple activities like reading and gaming can shape and reshape our brain.
