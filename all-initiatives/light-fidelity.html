Light Fidelity (Li-Fi)

Sending music through light.

A working proof-of-concept showing how data, in this case audio, can travel not through radio waves, but through rapidly modulated light.

Core Principle

How this demo works

The laptop plays a song and sends the audio waveform into the LED side of the circuit. Those LEDs then modulate their brightness at audio-rate, so quickly that the flicker is invisible, but the information is still being carried.

That light falls onto the solar panel receiver. Because the intensity of the light changes with the music, the electrical output of the solar panel changes in the same pattern.

That recovered electrical signal is then routed to a speaker, where it is amplified and played back. In simple terms: music in, light out; light in, music out.

Concept

What is Li-Fi?

Li-Fi, or Light Fidelity, is a form of visible-light communication in which LEDs transmit information by varying their intensity faster than the eye can detect.

A light-sensitive receiver such as a photodiode or solar cell detects those changes and converts them back into electrical signals that represent data. In this build, that data was audio. In more advanced systems, it could be text, images, or even high-speed internet.

Proof of concept

This build was intentionally simple so the idea stayed visible and understandable, while still demonstrating the core principle behind Li-Fi.

Why It Matters

Why Li-Fi can be useful

RF-free zones

Useful in spaces where radio communication may be restricted or may cause interference.

Security by containment

Because light usually does not pass through walls, communication can be more localized.

Large unlicensed spectrum

Visible and near-infrared light offer an enormous communication space compared to crowded RF bands.

High density

Each light source can become its own micro-cell, which can reduce congestion in crowded spaces.

Lighting + data

Existing lighting infrastructure can also function as data infrastructure in the right systems.

Reality Check

Trade-offs compared to Wi-Fi

Li-Fi is exciting, but it also comes with practical constraints. Because light can be blocked, placement matters much more than in radio-based systems. Bright ambient lighting and sunlight can introduce noise, and mobility between light cells requires careful design.

That means Li-Fi does not simply replace Wi-Fi everywhere. Instead, it becomes interesting in places where the unique strengths of light-based communication really matter.

Applications

Where Li-Fi can shine

Hospitals & aircraft

Settings where RF interference matters and communication needs more control.

Factories & power plants

Heavy electromagnetic environments where radio communication can be difficult.

Underwater communication

Over short distances, light can outperform radio in water-based environments.

Smart lighting & IoT

Lights can do more than illuminate, they can also help carry information.

Museums, libraries, classrooms

Localized communication tied to specific spaces or individual lighting zones.

High-density venues

Conference halls, airports, and large public spaces where localized cells may help reduce congestion.

What I Built

A small build with a big idea behind it

This was a minimal end-to-end Li-Fi chain: audio in, light out; light in, audio out. It was not optimized for long range or perfect fidelity, but that was never the point.

The point was to make the concept visible, understandable, and real in a single working build. That is what made this project meaningful to me. It turned a futuristic idea into something I could physically demonstrate.
