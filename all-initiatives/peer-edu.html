Learning to lead through awareness

Being a Peer Educator was not only about giving speeches. It was about learning how to speak to students about issues that actually affect their lives: safety, health, stress, relationships, peer pressure, personal growth, and the courage to ask for help when needed.

Through this role, I got the opportunity to participate in training sessions, conduct awareness activities, host interactive discussions, and help create a more informed and supportive school environment. It helped me understand that leadership is not always loud or formal. Sometimes, it begins with starting the right conversation.

What I worked on

These initiatives covered safety, health, mental well-being, inclusivity, environment, and life skills through student-led awareness.

Safety Awareness

Women Helpline Number 181

On January 31, 2025, an awareness session was held to educate students about the Women Helpline 181. We explained that this 24/7 toll-free service provides immediate assistance to women facing threats, unsafe situations, or distress by connecting them to police, legal aid, or counselling.

Real-life scenarios were used to show why knowing and using this helpline can matter in emergencies.

Child Safety

Child Helpline Number 1098

We also conducted an awareness campaign on Childline 1098, India’s 24-hour toll-free emergency helpline for children under 18. Students learned how it supports children facing abuse, neglect, family disputes, academic pressure, or emotional distress.

The session highlighted confidentiality, accessibility even without phone balance, and the importance of saving and sharing the number.

Environment & Health

Plantation Drive

The plantation drive focused on planting Ashwagandha, a medicinal plant known for its health and environmental benefits. Students were informed about its role in reducing stress, boosting immunity, and supporting well-being as per Ayurveda.

The drive also emphasized sustainability, soil conservation, biodiversity, and the importance of herbal medicine.

Student Well-being

Interactive Session with Classes 9 and 10

A seminar was hosted by the peer educators for Classes 9 and 10 on issues such as peer pressure, academics, substance abuse, personal growth, parent-child relationships, and time management.

The discussion encouraged students to make independent choices, handle academic stress, avoid unhealthy influences, communicate better with parents, and focus on self-growth.

Inclusivity

Transgender Awareness Camp at SD College

The Transgender Awareness Camp at SD College, Sector 32, Chandigarh, focused on legal rights, identity recognition, welfare access, housing rights, healthcare, and social inclusion.

The session also discussed the need for support groups and anti-discrimination cells in schools to promote dignity and acceptance.

Training

Training Session at Government Hospital, Sector 16

On October 18, 2024, peer educators attended a training session at Government Hospital, Sector 16, Chandigarh. Led by doctors including Dr. Paramjoti, Dr. Swati, Dr. Nancy, and Dr. Sonia, the session covered holistic health, eye care, substance abuse, mental health, anaemia, and hygiene.

We were also trained to conduct eye and haemoglobin tests in school for early awareness and detection.

Bodily Autonomy

My Body, My Rules

“My body, my rules” was an awareness message about bodily autonomy, consent, dignity, and personal freedom. The session emphasized that every individual has the right to make decisions about their own body without pressure, control, or judgment.

Health Camp

Eye Checkup Camp

The peer educators conducted an eye checkup camp for students at Sacred Heart Sr. Sec. School, Sector 26, Chandigarh. The aim was to identify vision-related issues early and encourage students to prioritize health.

The camp was organized systematically, with smooth coordination and active student participation.

CBSE Program

Adolescent Peer Educators Leadership Training

The CBSE-organized training program focused on life skills, health, well-being, family bonding, caring communication, healthy interpersonal relationships, and responsible digital behaviour.

Through three days of learning, students explored decision-making, problem-solving, empathy, conflict resolution, and how peer educators can create positive change within school communities.

Mental Health

Talk on Exam Fear, Stress, and Self-Comparison

On February 14, 2025, an awareness session was conducted to help students manage exam fear, stress, and self-comparison. We discussed practical strategies such as revision techniques, stress management, and focusing on personal growth rather than constant comparison.

The session encouraged students to approach exams with a healthier mindset and to measure progress against their own improvement.

What this role taught me

My Peer Educator journey taught me that awareness can be powerful when it is simple, practical, and student-led. The role helped me become more confident in speaking about sensitive issues and more thoughtful about how students experience pressure, fear, safety, and personal growth.

More than anything, it showed me that leadership is not only about holding a position. It is also about being willing to listen, start conversations, and help people feel informed, supported, and less alone.
