EARLY IDEA • SUSTAINABILITY • HEALTHCARE

Medicine Dispenser

One of my earliest ideas from Class 8.

There are so many ailments in this world. Medicines to cure or reduce the effect of these ailments come in plastic bottles and wraps. Plastic is non-biodegradable. Now, if plastics are said to be recyclable, it is still hard to collect bits of plastic, like that of medicines, so recycling it all is a far-fetched dream. It gets buried, affecting humans indirectly. Medicines sold in the present system are even costlier than they should be. So, I realised the need for something simpler and more helpful.

My project, a medicine dispenser, attends to this problem. As per my idea, a medicine dispenser, somehow easily made moisture-free, shall be installed in pharmacy shops. When in need, a person may fill capsules in a small glass box easily available at their place. When again in need, the person may refill the same box once again from nearby pharmacy shops.

Even if we feel like we should ignore the dispenser idea but still reduce the use of plastic, we cannot conclude that all medicines should be manufactured in glass bottles. If we plan to do so, we will be wasting a lot. For medicines like those for hypertension, hypothyroidism, and others which are regularly used, we can use just one dispenser for many people lifelong rather than hundreds of bottles.

Seeing the population that intakes such fast-selling medicines, we could save nearly 30 million bottles in two months in one shot for each such medicine. It is best suited for generic medicines. This even makes medicines cheaper for the public. Dispensers can be easily installed in hospitals for poor patients. We could also reduce the use of plastics and aluminium in certain areas of the pharmaceutical department.

- KESHIKA VERMA
